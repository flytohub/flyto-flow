# Licensing Strategy

The current repository license is Apache License 2.0. It permits modification,
redistribution, and commercial use under its terms. The trademark policy can
prevent a modified distribution from presenting itself as the official Flyto2
product, but it cannot turn an open-source copyright license into a ban on
forking or resale.

If the project must remain OSI open source, choose an OSI-approved license and
use trademark enforcement, a hosted service, support, and downstream proprietary
features as the commercial boundary. AGPL can require source availability for a
modified network service, but it does not prohibit commercial resale.

If the business requirement is to prohibit competing hosted use or resale, a
source-available license such as the Business Source License may express that
restriction, but the result must not be marketed as OSI open source.

Do not change the license casually or retroactively. A license change needs an
inventory of copyright holders, contributor consent where required, a clear
effective version, and review by qualified counsel. This document records the
product decision boundary; it is not legal advice.
